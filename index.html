What is TSM?

Team SoloMid (TSM) is a professional esports organization based in the United States. It was founded in September 2009 by Andy "Reginald" Dinh. TSM currently fields players in League of Legends, Apex Legends, Hearthstone, Super Smash Bros., PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds, Fortnite, Rocket League, Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Siege, and Magic: The Gathering Arena. The organization previously had a Counter-Strike: Global Offensive team based in Denmark and also briefly owned an Overwatch team.
Team SoloMid was founded by Reginald in September 2009 as a League of Legends community website and playing guide resource. In 2011, the organization began fielding a team in professional LoL competitions. The original roster consisted of Reginald, SaintVicious, Chaox, TheOddOne, and Locodoco. Reginald retired from competitive play at the end of 2013, opting to run the business full-time.

Joshua ‘Dardoch’ Hartnett is TSM’s starting jungler for Season 10 of the LCS, signing with the organization at the end of November 2019. Dardoch is as talented as he is notorious; despite being recognized as one of the most talented junglers in North America since his rookie split, he is also known as one of the region’s most contentious players. After a disastrous split on Team Liquid where his brashness and hunger to win put him at odds with his teammates and coaches, his career has been shrouded with infamy. Nevertheless, such a reputation only makes Dardoch the ideal TSM jungler - the only LCS position with enough notoriety to match him. With his top tier mechanical skill and jungle sense, perhaps he will be able to find vindication on TSM - not just for himself, but for the role he’s taken up as well.

Sergen ‘BrokenBlade’ Çelik joined TSM in the preseason leading up to Season 9 of the NA LCS to be the team’s starting top laner. Broken Blade was signed to the team after a year of complete domination within the Turkish Championship League, taking 1st or 2nd place in every major competition within the region and finishing the season with four accounts in challenger on the EUW servers. With this success at his young age, mechanically gifted top laner is coming into the NA LCS hungry for more, hoping to prove to the world that he can carry over his same level of dominance into a major region.

Currently the longest tenured member on the TSM League of Legends team, Søren ‘Bjergsen’ Bjerg is one of the cornerstones of the team’s success since he joined in 2013. The Danish mid laner has been hailed as a genius for much of his career, having been a shining star who excels in all aspects of the game. His consistent dominance in North America has led to many to regard him as one the best mid laner in the West, commanding great respect from analysts and competitors internationally.

Kasper ‘Kobbe’ Kobberup joined TSM in November 2019 to be the AD Carry for their primary LCS Roster. Kobbe is well known as one of the best AD Carries for the LEC, having been instrumental in Splyce’s successes over the past four years. Unlike many “consistent” AD Carries who sit back and farm, Kobbe has historically gained his own leads, often pushing the limit and making his own plays to help his team win games. Despite qualifying for Worlds and making it past groups, he’s hungry for more. Publicly stating that he “only wants to play if he has an actual chance of being #1”, Kobbe is seeking new fortunes as he crosses the Atlantic to play for TSM, seeking that championship title that has slipped through his hands time and time again.

Vincent ‘Biofrost’ Wang is the starting Support for TSM’s main League of Legends roster going into 2020. A familiar name to many of our fans - Biofrost was introduced to the LCS in Summer of 2016, playing his rookie split on TSM during which the team finished top of the league with a 17-1 record and steamrolled the competition during playoffs. Following that split, he stayed on the team for an additional year, never losing a single best of 5 series and winning two more playoffs with the organization. Biofrost is returning to TSM for season 10 after spending the past two years on CLG, during which his leadership skills developed greatly. Along with his ability to cleanly execute plays with high mechanical requirements in clutch moments, Biofrost’s return is an exciting moment for all parties involved.
